My Global Journey

My passion for understanding different cultures began when I navigated the night markets of Xi'an alone at 15, observing how local vendors adapted their sales tactics for different customers. That curiosity blossomed into a professional lens - I now analyze business challenges through the perspective of someone who's experienced consumer behavior in 14 countries across 3 continents.

At NYU Stern, I've merged my love for travel with rigorous training in data science. Leading the Women in Business mentorship program taught me how cultural context influences professional development needs - insights I gained from working with students from 22 different countries. My research collaboration in France revealed how ethical decision-making frameworks vary across markets, while my sneaker resale venture demonstrated how localized data can reveal untapped opportunities.

I'm particularly drawn to roles where I can leverage both my analytical skills and global perspective to help businesses understand diverse consumer bases. Whether analyzing retail patterns from Paris to Shanghai or comparing digital adoption rates in emerging markets, I believe the most compelling insights emerge when data meets cultural context.
